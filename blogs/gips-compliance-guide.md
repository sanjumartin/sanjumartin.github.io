# GIPS Compliance: A Practical Guide for Asset Managers

**Category:** GIPS | **Date:** June 1, 2026 | **Read time:** 7 min

---

The Global Investment Performance Standards (GIPS) represent the gold standard for investment performance reporting. Having worked with GIPS compliance across multiple global firms — AXA Investment Managers, BNY Mellon, and BNP Paribas — I've seen first-hand how it transforms the way organisations communicate performance to clients.

## What Are GIPS Standards?

GIPS is a set of ethical standards for calculating and presenting investment performance. They are governed by the CFA Institute and adopted voluntarily by investment management firms worldwide. Compliance signals to clients and prospects that your performance figures are:

- **Accurate** — calculated using standardised methodologies
- **Complete** — presenting composites fairly across all fee-paying accounts
- **Comparable** — enabling like-for-like comparisons across firms

## Why GIPS Compliance Matters

For asset managers competing for institutional mandates, GIPS compliance is increasingly non-negotiable. Pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, and large institutional investors routinely require GIPS-compliant performance records before awarding mandates.

Beyond client acquisition, GIPS drives internal discipline:

1. **Data governance** — forces clean, auditable data pipelines
2. **Process standardisation** — reduces errors in performance calculation
3. **Transparency** — builds a culture of accountability within teams

## Key Requirements to Get Started

### 1. Define Your Firm
Clearly define what constitutes "the firm" — all assets managed by the investment management entity for which you are claiming compliance.

### 2. Create Composites
Group all discretionary, fee-paying portfolios with similar investment strategies into composites. Every portfolio must belong to at least one composite.

### 3. Calculate Returns
Use time-weighted returns (TWR) to eliminate the impact of external cash flows. Sub-period returns must be geometrically linked.

### 4. Maintain Records
GIPS requires a minimum of 5 years of compliant history (or since firm inception if less), building to a 10-year track record.

### 5. Annual Verification
While not mandatory, third-party verification (e.g., by PWC or KPMG) significantly enhances credibility. During my time at AXA IM, assisting with annual PWC GIPS audits was a core part of our governance process.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Composite construction errors** — incorrectly including or excluding portfolios
- **Benchmark selection** — using inappropriate benchmarks that make composites look favourable
- **Incomplete disclosure** — failing to disclose composite dispersion, number of portfolios, or asset levels
- **Fee treatment** — not clearly distinguishing gross-of-fees vs net-of-fees returns

## Closing Thoughts

GIPS compliance is not merely a regulatory checkbox — it is a commitment to transparency and professionalism. Teams that embrace GIPS as a quality framework (rather than a burden) consistently deliver better client outcomes and stronger audit results.

If you are beginning your GIPS journey or looking to strengthen an existing programme, the most important step is securing buy-in from senior leadership. Without it, the data governance and process changes required will face constant friction.
